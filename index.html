🔪 Prep Steps 0/0

  1. Preheat oven to 425 °F (220 °C)

    Line two large baking sheets with parchment paper. Place wire racks on top of each sheet for maximum airflow and crunch.

  2. Pound & slice chicken

    Pound to an even 3/4-inch thickness. Slice into 6 equal cutlets. Pat completely dry with paper towels — moisture = soggy coating.

  3. Season the chicken

    Mix all spices together. Rub 2/3 of the spice mix directly onto both sides of each cutlet.

  4. Set up 3-stage dredging station

    Bowl 1: Flour + cornstarch + remaining spice mix. Bowl 2: Eggs whisked with 2 tbsp water. Bowl 3: Panko + Parmesan + lemon zest.

  5. Dredge all cutlets

    Flour → egg → panko. Press panko firmly. Place on wire rack. Spray generously with avocado oil on both sides. Do NOT skip the oil.

  6. Start the rice

    Rinse 3 cups rice until water runs clear. Add to pot with 6 cups chicken broth. Boil, cover, reduce to low, cook 18 min. Steam 5 min covered.

  7. Prep vegetables

    Cut broccoli into equal-size florets. Slice peppers and zucchini into 1/2-inch pieces. Toss with 1 tbsp avocado oil, salt, pepper. Spread on baking sheet.

⏱️ Time-save: Start the rice and veg sheet at the same time the oven preheats. Everything finishes together.

🔥 Cook Steps 0/0

  1. Load oven — Chicken top rack, veg bottom

    Place chicken racks on the top oven shelf. Put the vegetable sheet on the bottom rack for maximum radiant heat from above.

  2. Bake 12 minutes — flip chicken

    After 12 minutes, flip each chicken cutlet with tongs. The bottom should be golden. Spray avocado oil after flipping.

  3. Toss vegetables at 12-min mark

    While flipping the chicken, also toss the vegetables on the bottom rack so they brown evenly on both sides.

  4. Bake another 10–13 minutes

    Continue baking until chicken reaches 165 °F (74 °C) and the panko is deep golden brown. Vegetables should be tender with charred edges.

  5. Rest the chicken — 5 minutes

    Remove from oven. Let rest on the rack for 5 minutes — do NOT cover. This keeps the crust crispy and lets juices redistribute.

  6. Make dipping sauce

    Mix: 1/2 cup Greek yogurt + 1 tbsp lemon juice + 1/2 tsp garlic powder + salt + pepper. Divide into 6 small sauce cups.

  7. Drizzle hot honey glaze

    Warm hot honey 20 seconds in microwave. Lightly drizzle over each chicken cutlet for flavor without sogginess.

📦 Assemble & Store 0/0

  1. Fluff and portion rice

    Fluff the rice with a fork. Portion about 3/4 cup cooked rice into the bottom of each of the 6 meal prep containers. Season lightly with salt and a squeeze of lemon juice.

  2. Add roasted vegetables

    Divide the roasted broccoli, bell peppers, and zucchini evenly among all 6 containers. Place next to the rice.

  3. Slice & add chicken

    Slice each rested chicken cutlet diagonally into 3–4 strips. Fan the strips on top of the rice in each container.

  4. Add sauce cup & garnish

    Nestle the yogurt dipping sauce cup in the corner of each container. Add a lemon wedge and a pinch of smoked paprika as garnish.

  5. Cool before sealing

    Leave containers open for 15–20 minutes at room temperature to cool. Sealing hot containers causes condensation that makes the coating soggy.

  6. Seal & label containers

    Seal each container. Label with today's date. Refrigerate up to 5 days. Freeze up to 3 months (freeze without sauce).

♨️ Reheating Guide

🔥 Oven (best)

400 °F for 8–10 min on a wire rack. Keeps coating crispy.

💨 Air Fryer

375 °F for 5–7 min. Nearly as crispy as fresh.

📡 Microwave

2 min on high. Quick but coating softens. Use paper towel on top.
