🔪 Prep Steps 0/0

  1. Cook & cool the rice

    Cook the 2 cups of dry rice. Spread the cooked rice out on a baking sheet and refrigerate until completely cool. (Cold rice fries up perfectly without turning to mush).

  2. Dice & marinate chicken

    Dice 60oz thawed chicken breast into bite-sized pieces. In a large bowl, mix chicken with 4 tbsp soy sauce, garlic salt, black pepper, smoked paprika, and 2 whole eggs.

  3. Set up cornstarch station

    Pour the cornstarch into a large, wide, sealable Tupperware container.

  4. The Double Dip

    Drop a handful of wet chicken into the cornstarch, seal and shake. Remove, dip rapidly back into the wet marinade bowl, then drop back into the cornstarch and shake again for a thick crust.

🔥 Cook Steps 0/0

  1. Load the Air Fryer

    Place double-coated chicken into the air fryer basket in a single layer (may require multiple batches). Spray generously with cooking spray so no dry white powder remains.

  2. Air fry 15-17 minutes

    Air fry at 350 °F for 15-17 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden and violently crispy.

  3. Sauté vegetables & eggs

    Heat 1 tbsp sesame oil in a large wok. Toss in 2.5 cups frozen Kirkland veggies. Cook 3-4 mins. Push to one side, pour in 3 whole eggs + 1 cup egg whites. Scramble, then mix with veggies.

  4. Finish the fried rice

    Dump the cold cooked rice and 2 tbsp soy sauce into the wok. Toss vigorously for 3-5 minutes until heated through and toasted. Remove from heat.

  5. Simmer the glaze

    In a saucepan, combine all glaze ingredients (soy sauce, rice vinegar, gochujang, brown sugar, garlic, ginger, honey). Simmer on medium-low for 3-5 minutes until it thickens to a syrup.

📦 Assemble & Store 0/0

  1. Portion the fried rice

    Divide the massive batch of egg-white vegetable fried rice evenly among the 5 glass meal prep containers.

  2. Add the crispy chicken

    Divide the ~45oz of cooked crispy chicken evenly (approx 9oz per container) and place it directly on top of the rice.

  3. Separate the glaze

    To keep the crust crunchy all week, do NOT sauce the chicken now. Portion the thickened glaze evenly into 5 small condiment cups.

  4. Cool before sealing

    Leave containers open for 15–20 minutes at room temperature to cool. Sealing hot containers traps steam and will completely ruin the crispy coating.

  5. Seal & store

    Seal each container and refrigerate. When ready to eat, microwave chicken and rice for 2-3 minutes, then pour the separate cold/room-temp sauce over the hot chicken.
